Part 1 - Project Background

Context on the project’s origins, and summarize the progress that the team made before I became more deeply involved as a producer during Alpha Development.


What is Reality Crisis?

Reality Crisis is a remote game development studio based out of Helsinki, Finland. It was founded by Kasimir Lehto (CEO) and Petri Kajander (Business Development). Together, they envision a catalogue of pioneering Augmented Reality (AR) mobile games that can simultaneously tap into the action sports lifestyle and social gaming markets. The studio’s flagship title, Skatrix, is an AR mobile skateboarding game that lets players project their skater avatar onto the real world. Or, as Kasimir put it when we first met, “imagine that all the world’s a skate park”.



I first became involved with Reality Crisis and the development of Skatrix as a freelance production consultant during the Fall of 2018. The company’s goal at the time was simply to produce a viable alpha prototype of an AR skateboarding game, to be presented as a project proposal for the Finnish Tech Fund. This prototype was to be designed by Kasimir, in collaboration with veteran action sports game designer and producer, Jack Rebbetoy, who had worked on the Tony Hawk’s Pro Skater franchise from 200X to 20XX.

By the Spring of 2019, the founding team had secured seed funding to kick off R&D for the project, and contracted MideaLab, a Ukranian development shop located in Lviv, to provide development services. I assisted Kasimir with researching the technology, and with developer interviews to assemble the team. Later, I was requested to serve as the team’s agile coach.



Recap

My Role: From the Fall of 2018 to the Spring of 2019, I served as a production consultant for Reality Crisis, and later as an Agile Coach for a newly formed, partially remote, R&D team.
